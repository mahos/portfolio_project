A little more about myself...

Born in Japan, raised as the only child in a nomadic researcher family, I moved 8 times across the Pacific or the Atlantic Ocean throughout my childhood. I have stayed in the USA since high school for roughly 20 years now.

I have a soft spot for helping immigrants or brain injury survivors with language barriers and communicating through universal language (aka music - talk to me about whether music is really a universal language and I will entertain you about it for hours).

What drives me is the opportunity to be creative with solutions for those in need - and I have professionally used music and instruments as tools to problem solve those with therapeutic needs. Ever since 2012, when I decided to learn programming to create a GUI to collect music therapy patients’ data for their patterned rhythmic responses, I’ve been hooked to the idea of providing “repeatable” resources and tools via the web browser.
